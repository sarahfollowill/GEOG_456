Bar charts are a great visualization to show variation of a single phenomenon over time, even if there is no general trend to the data. These charts are limited, however, in that if there is too much data, it could be hard to interpret, and if there is not enough data, it could be difficult to determine any conclusions from the visualization. It is also not the most aesthetically pleasing chart based on its blocky nature that lacks flow.

Here, I displayed my data on the rise of urbanization in Atlanta, GA through a bar chart which allowed for a simple way to see how urban area has expanded in the past few decades. I also chose the reds colorscale to show urbanization becoming more extreme over time as the red becomes darker.
