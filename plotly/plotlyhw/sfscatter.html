Scatterplots are very basic, but useful charts that are perfect for showing a data trend over time via points, as is seen in my data. A scatterplot might not be as useful, though, when there is not a general trend to the data over time, or if there is not enough data to determine a trend. Here, I used a scatterplot to effectively show the exponential increase in urbanized area in Atlanta, GA.

I chose to use the Portland colorscale to show urbanization getting more extreme over time from a cool blue to an aggressive red on the upper end of the data. Additionally, the reds colorscale which I will use in other charts made it more difficult to see the smaller values.
